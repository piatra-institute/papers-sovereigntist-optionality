# Brief

## Question

When a sovereigntist (nationalist-populist) party becomes a credible route to power in a semi-peripheral EU economy, does capital "move to the far right," and if that phrasing is wrong, what is the right question?

## Claim

"Capital moves to the far right" hides its unit. Capital is a population of firms whose state-dependence $s$ and mobility $m$ differ, and those two quantities decide the response to a credible-but-uncertain sovereigntist government arriving with probability $p$. The right object is a real option: state-dependent local capital buys a call on reallocated state rents (hedge/access); mobile capital exercises its exit option (prices risk); immobile market-exposed capital lobbies (voice). The model locates a state-dependence threshold $s^{*}$ where hedge overtakes exit, shows the aggregate direction of capital is an artifact of fraction composition (so a bond sell-off and an access rush coincide), and proves the identification result: a cross-section of access holdings cannot distinguish option-buying from capture; only the response to $dp$ can.
