## Findings

### The phenomenon (reported figures; motivate, do not calibrate)

- [T4] After the May 2025 first-round nationalist win in Romania, the leu broke past 5/euro and fell ~3%, 10-year yields rose ~60bp, a debt auction was cut, S&P flagged investment-grade risk — Reuters/Fitch reporting via the seed deep-research reports — motivates §1 the "exit/risk-pricing" channel.
- [T4] Romanian 2025 party finance: ~RON 232m subsidies, ~RON 112m loans, ~RON 692k corporate donations; ~48% of subsidy spending on press/propaganda; cross-party financiers (PSD donors in AUR revenues) — Expert Forum / EPDE via seed — motivates §1 the "access/hedge" channel and the cross-party salvage parameter $\phi$.
- [T4] Comparative single-bid procurement shares (EC Rule of Law 2025): Poland 54%, Romania 45%, Bulgaria 37%, Slovakia 33%, Hungary 32%, Czechia 40% — where state discretion over allocation is thick enough for access to have contractual meaning (the $s$ / $\delta$ channel).
- [T4] Stage contrast: Hungary (consolidated, wide discretion, funds suspended pending reform), Poland (post-PiS market relief on EU-ties restoration, ~EUR 137bn blocked funds), Italy (mainstreamed; spread <100bp once seen as constrained) — maps to the three regimes in §5.

### Theory (T1/T2; load-bearing)

- [T1] Hirschman (1970) Exit, Voice, and Loyalty — the strategy menu; the paper adds "hedge" as a third option (buy into the feared reallocation). §2.
- [T1] McDonald & Siegel (1986); Dixit & Pindyck (1994); Trigeorgis (1996) — option value of waiting / real options: irreversible commitment under uncertainty obeys a threshold rule. Frames access as a call and the threshold $s^{*}$. §1, §3.
- [T1] Pástor & Veronesi (2013); Kelly, Pástor & Veronesi (2016); Julio & Yook (2012); Jens (2017) — political uncertainty commands a risk premium, depresses investment, priced in option markets. The exit/risk channel and the "event study on access" program. §1, §6.
- [T2] Wallerstein (2004); Nölke & Vliegenthart (2009); Bohle & Greskovits (2012); Schedelik et al. (2021) — semi-periphery / dependent market economies: why these economies hold both fractions at once. §1.
- [T2] Hillman, Keim & Schuler (2004); Bonardi, Hillman & Keim (2005); Bonardi, Holburn & Vanden Bergh (2006) — firms invest in political access in proportion to political (vs market) exposure; regulated/procurement firms invest most. Grounds the $s$ axis. §2.
- [T2] Hellman, Jones & Kaufmann (2003); Shleifer & Vishny (1994); Innes (2014); Markus (2015); Mungiu-Pippidi (2015); Magyar (2016) — state capture / politicians-and-firms / rents: the "capture" data-generating process that the cross-section cannot distinguish from optionality. §6.
- [T2] Kelemen (2017); Scheppele (2018); Appel & Orenstein (2018) — EU rule-of-law / conditionality as the discipline on rent-discretion $\delta$, the model's most sensitive parameter. §2, §5, §7.
- [T2] Gherghina & Volintiru (2017) — clientelism, public resources, private contributors; cross-party contribution patterns: the salvage value $\phi$ of access. §3.
- [T2] Calvo (1998); Jensen (2008) — sudden stops / FDI political economy: the macro form of the exit channel. §3.
- [T2] Hall & Soskice (2001) — varieties of capitalism: ideal-type method, not fitting. §8.

### Generalization: the space of regimes (§7)

- [T1] Mehlum, Moene & Torvik (2006), *Economic Journal* 116(508):1-20 — institutions, not rent size, decide the resource curse (producer- vs grabber-friendly). The **Norway lower boundary**: institutions cap $\delta$ from the inside, so optionality is null despite vast rents. Verified (DOI 10.1111/j.1468-0297.2006.01045.x).
- [T1] Beblawi & Luciani (1987), *The Rentier State* (Croom Helm) — rentier-state theory: the state distributes exogenous (oil) rent, the private sector depends on the distribution. The **Gulf upper boundary**: $\delta$ near total but $p\to1$, so access is tribute, not a bet. Verified (Taylor & Francis / Routledge reissue).
- [T2] Pearson, Rithmire & Tsai (2021), *Current History* 120(827):207-213 — "party-state capitalism": the Party disciplines private capital; access to the party becomes a survival condition. The **China coordinate** (high $\delta$, $p\to1$). Verified (UC Press; HBS WP 21-065).
- Russia: Markus (2015), already cited — exit blocked, tribute under predation. US transactional: Hillman, Keim & Schuler (2004), already cited — a thin high-$s$ fraction hedges while the mobile bulk accommodates ("state welfare for capital").
